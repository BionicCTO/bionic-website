The biggest threat to health insurers isn't a competitor. It's a category shift that most boards can't see because they're measuring the wrong things.

## The five threats

Here are five ways AI is already reshaping the competitive landscape for health insurers — and why traditional competitive intelligence won't catch them.

### 1. The advisor becomes the insurer

AI-powered health platforms are building direct relationships with your customers. When a consumer trusts an AI health advisor for daily decisions, the insurer becomes a payment rail — invisible, commoditised, and replaceable.

### 2. Risk selection becomes real-time

Traditional underwriting relies on actuarial tables and historical data. AI-native competitors are building real-time risk models that update continuously. The accuracy gap compounds daily.

### 3. Claims become invisible

The most AI-advanced health systems are moving toward automatic claims processing — where the claim is generated, assessed, and paid without human intervention. Insurers that still run 10-day claims cycles will lose customers not to competitors, but to platforms where claims simply disappear.

### 4. Prevention beats payout

AI health monitoring means some companies will prevent the conditions that generate claims. When prevention is cheaper than treatment, the entire insurance value proposition inverts.

### 5. Regulation becomes the moat — then the trap

Health insurers assume regulation protects them. It does — for now. But regulation also means they're slower to adopt AI, creating an innovation debt that compounds while unregulated competitors build capability.

## What to do about it

The first step is honest assessment. Not "are we doing AI?" but "are we building at the pace our competitors are?"

Most boards will find the answer uncomfortable.
