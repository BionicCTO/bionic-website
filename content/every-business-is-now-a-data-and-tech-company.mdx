The phrase "every company is a tech company" has been repeated so often it's lost its meaning. But the underlying truth is more urgent than ever — and most boards still haven't internalised it.

## The shift that matters

Here's the distinction that matters: it's not that your company *uses* technology. It's that your company's competitive advantage is now inseparable from how well it generates, processes, and acts on data.

This isn't a technology conversation. It's a governance one.

## What boards get wrong

Most boards treat AI as a technology project. Something for the CTO to manage. A line item in the digital transformation budget.

**That's like treating electricity as a facilities issue.**

AI is reshaping the operating model itself — who makes decisions, how fast they're made, what data informs them, and who (or what) executes them.

## The mental model that's missing

The boards that will thrive in the next decade are the ones that understand three things:

- **Data is capital** — it appreciates, compounds, and requires governance like any other asset on the balance sheet
- **AI is labour** — it performs work, makes decisions, and needs oversight structures just like your human workforce
- **Speed is structural** — the gap between organisations that sense-and-respond in real time and those that rely on quarterly cycles will become unbridgeable

## What this means for you

If your board hasn't had a dedicated session on AI strategy — not a vendor demo, not a "digital update" buried in AOB — you're already behind.

The question isn't whether AI will reshape your industry. It's whether your board will be literate enough to govern the transition.
