There's a comforting myth in corporate governance: that caution is free. That waiting for more data, more proof, more certainty is the prudent choice.

The evidence says otherwise.

## The cost of waiting

McKinsey's latest research on AI adoption shows that organisations which delayed AI investment by 18-24 months experienced a measurable erosion in competitive position that took 3-5 years to recover — if they recovered at all.

BCG's analysis is even more pointed: **the gap between AI leaders and laggards is widening at an accelerating rate.** The compounding nature of AI advantage means that every month of delay makes the catch-up exponentially harder.

## Why boards hesitate

Board-level hesitation on AI typically comes from three sources:

- **Incomplete understanding** — only 26% of board directors report adequate AI literacy to govern AI programmes effectively
- **Risk framing** — boards frame AI as a risk to manage rather than a capability to build, which biases toward inaction
- **False equivalence** — treating "doing nothing" as a neutral position, when it's actually the highest-risk strategy

## The MIT finding

MIT Sloan's research reveals something counter-intuitive: **organisations that made imperfect AI investments early outperformed those that waited for perfect information.** The learning advantage from early deployment was more valuable than the efficiency gain from waiting.

## What 80% of boards are missing

Perhaps the most striking statistic: 80% of boards admit their oversight structures are inadequate for AI governance. This isn't a technology gap — it's a governance gap. And it's the governance gap that creates the strategic paralysis.

## The reframe

The question for every board should shift from "are we ready for AI?" to "what is the cost of not being ready?"

When you quantify the cost of inaction — in competitive position, talent attraction, operational efficiency, and strategic optionality — waiting stops looking prudent. It starts looking like the most expensive strategy available.
