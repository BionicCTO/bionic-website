The adoption numbers look impressive. 78% of organisations report using AI in some capacity. Boards see the dashboards, hear the updates, tick the boxes.

But dig one layer deeper and the picture changes dramatically.

## The 4% problem

Only 4% of organisations generate strategic value from their AI investments. Not operational efficiency. Not cost reduction. Strategic value — the kind that changes competitive position.

The gap between 78% and 4% is where most organisations live. They've adopted AI. They haven't transformed with it.

## What separates the 4%

The organisations generating strategic value from AI share three characteristics:

### 1. AI is embedded in decision-making, not reporting

Most organisations use AI to generate reports faster. The 4% use AI to make decisions differently. The distinction is subtle but fundamental: AI as a productivity tool vs. AI as a decision architecture.

### 2. The operating model changed, not just the tools

Adding AI to an existing operating model is like putting a jet engine on a bicycle. The 4% redesigned the operating model around AI capabilities — new team structures, new decision rights, new governance frameworks.

### 3. Speed of insight became a strategic metric

Traditional strategy operates on quarterly cycles. The 4% measure the time between insight generation and strategic action — and they've compressed it from months to hours.

## The reinvention gap

The gap between experimentation and reinvention is where competitive advantage lives. Most organisations are stuck in experimentation — running pilots, testing tools, building proof of concepts.

**Experimentation without reinvention is expensive learning that never compounds.**

The organisations that break through are the ones willing to ask: "If we were building this company from scratch today, with AI at the core, what would it look like?"

And then actually building toward that answer.
